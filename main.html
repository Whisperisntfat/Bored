THE LAST OF US

Survive. Together.

THE STORY

In a world devastated by a fungal pandemic that turns humans into violent creatures, hardened survivor Joel is hired to smuggle 14-year-old Ellie out of a quarantine zone.

What starts as a straightforward job soon transforms into a brutal journey across the post-apocalyptic United States, where they must rely on each other for survival.

Their journey forever bonds them as they fight off both infected and desperate survivors in a world gone mad.

CHARACTERS

Joel Miller

A hardened survivor who lost everything in the outbreak. His world is forever changed when he meets Ellie.

Ellie Williams

A brave teenager with a unique immunity to the infection. Her resilience and wit make her an unlikely companion for Joel.

Tommy Miller

Joel's younger brother and former soldier. He represents hope in a hopeless world after finding purpose in the Jackson community.

Marlene

The determined leader of the Fireflies resistance group who knew Ellie's mother. She tasks Joel with transporting Ellie, believing her immunity could lead to a cure.

TIMELINE

The Outbreak - 2013

Cordyceps fungus mutates and spreads rapidly, causing worldwide chaos and collapse of civilization.

Boston QZ - 2033

Joel works as a smuggler in the Boston quarantine zone. He's tasked with escorting Ellie outside the QZ.

The Journey Begins

Ellie reveals her immunity to Joel. They begin their cross-country journey to find the Fireflies.

Finding the Fireflies

After surviving countless dangers, Joel and Ellie finally reach the Fireflies in Salt Lake City.

ABOUT THE SERIES

The Last of Us is a critically acclaimed action-adventure game developed by Naughty Dog and published by Sony Computer Entertainment.

First released in 2013 for PlayStation 3, it was remastered for PlayStation 4 in 2014. A television adaptation premiered on HBO in 2023, starring Pedro Pascal as Joel and Bella Ramsey as Ellie.

The story explores themes of love, loss, survival, and what it means to be human in a world stripped of humanity.
